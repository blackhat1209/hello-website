Lesson 1: Wave–Particle Duality

Wave–particle duality is a fundamental concept of quantum mechanics. It states that light and matter can behave both as waves and as particles.

Example

Light behaves like a wave when it produces interference patterns in experiments like the double slit experiment. However, in the photoelectric effect it behaves like particles called photons.

Key Formula

De Broglie wavelength:

λ = h / p

Where:

h = Planck's constant p = momentum of the particle

Quick Question

What does wave–particle duality mean?

Wave–particle duality means that particles like electrons and photons can behave both as waves and as particles depending on the experiment.
