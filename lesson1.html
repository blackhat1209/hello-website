Lesson 1: Wave-Particle Duality

Theory

Wave-particle duality states that light and matter can behave both as waves and as particles. Example: In the double slit experiment, light forms interference patterns like waves. But in the photoelectric effect, light behaves like particles called photons.

Question

Why does the photoelectric effect prove that light behaves like particles?

Because electrons are emitted only when light with sufficient frequency strikes the metal, showing that energy is delivered in discrete packets called photons.
