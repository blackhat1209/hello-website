Introduction to Quantum Physics

Quantum physics studies how matter and energy behave at extremely small scales such as atoms, electrons, and photons. Classical physics cannot fully explain these microscopic phenomena.

Atomic Structure

Atoms consist of a nucleus made of protons and neutrons with electrons orbiting around them. Quantum mechanics explains how electrons occupy specific energy levels instead of fixed classical orbits.

Wave–Particle Duality

One of the most surprising discoveries of quantum physics is that particles such as electrons and photons behave both like particles and waves. This phenomenon is known as wave–particle duality.

Double Slit Experiment

The double slit experiment demonstrates the wave nature of particles. When particles pass through two narrow slits, they create an interference pattern similar to waves.

Question: What does wave–particle duality mean?

Wave–particle duality means that particles such as electrons and photons exhibit both wave-like and particle-like behaviour depending on the experiment being performed.
