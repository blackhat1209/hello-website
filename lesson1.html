Lesson 1: Wave–Particle Duality

Wave–particle duality states that light and matter can behave both as waves and as particles.

Example

Light produces interference patterns like waves in the double slit experiment but behaves like particles in the photoelectric effect.

Formula

λ = h / p

h = Planck's constant p = momentum

Quick Question

What does wave–particle duality mean?

Wave–particle duality means particles such as electrons and photons can behave both as waves and as particles.
