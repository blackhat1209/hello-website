Introduction to Quantum Physics

Quantum physics studies how matter and energy behave at extremely small scales such as atoms, electrons, and photons.

Atomic Structure

Atoms consist of a nucleus made of protons and neutrons with electrons surrounding it. Quantum mechanics explains how electrons occupy specific energy levels around the nucleus.

Wave–Particle Duality

Particles such as electrons and photons behave both like waves and like particles depending on the experiment. This concept is called wave–particle duality.

Double Slit Experiment

The double slit experiment shows the wave nature of particles. When particles pass through two slits, they produce an interference pattern like waves.

Question: What does wave–particle duality mean?

Wave–particle duality means particles like electrons and photons can behave both as waves and as particles.
